Trout Fishing

Introduction

There are many different species of trout. Trout are mainly caught in the spring and fall. The three most common species are rainbow, brook, and brown trout. Trout are tyically a smaller profile fish and are aggressive. It is safer to grab these fish from there body rather than the mouth because of their teeth.

Where to find them

Trout are often found in streams and rivers. They like cold, fast moving water.

Types of Lures to Use and How to Fish Them

Tubes imitate zooplankton and other easy snacks all fish snack on. This lure is highly productive and you can reel it in steadily, twitch it in bursts, or use it like a jig and slowly flick it up. Swimbaits are great for strams and rivers with a strong current. Trout love to feast on smaller baitfish, especially bigger trout. The best way to trigger strikes when they are up feeding on baitfish is a paddletail swimbait that can disrupt the column. Just reel this bait in there isn't anything special to it. Small spinners are another great lure for trout fishing. This is similar to a swim bait but the flashing van tend to attract more fish.
